2. Casablanca Valley Wine Stop ▾
After a week in Santiago, we are heading to Valparaíso, an unparalleled art lover's paradise. On the way, we stopped in the Casablanca Valley — sitting perfectly midway between Santiago and Valparaíso, just 100 km from the capital, making it an absolutely perfect day trip. We visited the Casas del Bosque winery, toured the estate, and bought some bottles. This entire area is covered in vineyards producing famous Chilean white wines. The strong marine influence from the Pacific brings cool breezes, morning mist, and moderate temperatures — ideal for highly aromatic whites and fruity reds. We happily tasted some excellent Carmenere, Chardonnay, and crisp Sauvignon Blanc. Chilean Sauvignon Blanc is incredibly smooth, dangerously easy to drink, and perfect for summer.
這對夫婦參觀了Casas del Bosque酒莊，參觀了莊園並購買了幾瓶酒。他們品嚐了優秀的Carmenere、Chardonnay和清爽的Sauvignon Blanc。他們特別喜愛智利的Sauvignon Blanc，形容它極為順滑、好喝到危險，非常適合夏天。他們還注意到在標準的儲存槽和橡木桶之外，還使用了獨特的蛋形混凝土容器來釀酒。
3. Arriving in Valparaíso ▾
Located about 120 km northwest of Santiago on the central Chilean coast, Valparaíso is the country's second largest metropolitan area. Imagine a city that tumbles down 45 hills toward the Pacific Ocean, where every wall is a canvas, every staircase a mosaic, and every alleyway hides a story. Welcome to Valparaíso. You're not going to believe our room here. We were offered an upgrade, and of course we took it. It wasn't much — I think it was $30 for an upgrade, but we were shocked. A beautiful room with an incredible amount of space. And the view actually looks like the picture: the Pacific Ocean and Valparaíso, with Viña del Mar visible in the distance. This is a city where houses are painted in phosphorescent pinks, jade greens, and butter yellows. Where rickety funiculars from the 1880s still haul passengers up impossible inclines. And where pirates once plundered churches for gold. Valpo is a feeling — the smell of fresh empanadas drifting from a hillside kitchen, the sound of a street musician's guitar echoing through a cobblestone passage. Chaotic, gritty, poetic, and utterly alive.
Valparaíso被形容為無與倫比的藝術愛好者天堂。位於Santiago西北約120公里的智利中部海岸，是該國第二大都市區。這座城市沿著45座山丘向太平洋傾瀉而下，形成了戲劇性且色彩繽紛的景觀，數百年來一直啟發著藝術家和創意人士。
4. The Street Art Movement & Pinochet ▾
The street art movement bloomed in secret and in defiance. On September 11th, 1973, General Augusto Pinochet — born in Valparaíso in 1915 — seized power in a military coup. Within days, Pablo Neruda died under highly suspicious circumstances. The dictatorship banned all forms of political art. Those caught defying the order risked being disappeared, tortured, killed, and erased. But Valpo's narrow, hidden streets and winding stairways offered something Santiago's wide boulevards did not: concealment. An underground network of artists formed, painting powerful political messages on the walls of back alleys and forgotten passages. They worked at night, risked their lives, and watched their murals get painted over when discovered by military police. After Pinochet's fall, Valparaíso's local government did something remarkable and virtually unique in Chile: it legalized street art as a celebration of freedom of expression. Everywhere else in the country, graffiti and murals remain permitted only under formal commission.
在Valparaíso，每面牆都是一塊畫布，每段樓梯都是一幅馬賽克，每條小巷都藏著一個故事。這座城市充滿活力的街頭藝術場景世界聞名，色彩繽紛的壁畫遍布整個山丘社區的建築物。海洋背景、依附在陡峭山坡上的彩色房屋以及充滿藝術氣息的街道的結合，營造出智利其他任何地方都無法比擬的氛圍。
5. A Living Gallery ▾
In Valpo, the streets themselves became open galleries. The local government directly funded artists to create new works. Hotels, shops, and restaurants began competing to have the best murals on their walls. The result is a city where art is not confined to museums or galleries. You cannot walk 10 steps on Cerro Concepción without encountering a mural that stops you in your tracks. Entire buildings are wrapped in color. Staircases become mosaics. Retaining walls become portals into surrealist dreamscapes. These 29 steps are painted to resemble black and white piano keys, accompanied by murals of Beethoven himself. The piano stairs are just one of dozens of painted stairways. Victorian homes that once housed European merchants now lean precariously into ravines. It is beautiful precisely because it is imperfect.
這對夫婦住在一間擁有城市絕佳景觀的非凡房間裡。Valparaíso獨特的地理環境——建在俯瞰太平洋的45座陡峭山丘上——意味著幾乎從任何角度看到的景色都很壯觀。這座城市的波西米亞精神、藝術能量和戲劇性的環境使其成為南美洲最令人難忘的目的地之一。
6. Closing Thoughts ▾
Valparaíso is not a city you visit. It is a city that happens to you. It sneaks under your skin through its colors, its stories, its sounds, and its defiant creativity. A place where a devastating earthquake reshaped the city's geography, where the opening of a canal on the other side of the continent collapsed an economy, where a dictatorship tried to silence art and failed. Riding one of these creaking, century-old funiculars is a rite of passage for any visitor. Imaginative 'Imagine' for Description ▾
使用祈使句 'Imagine' 來引導觀眾進行想像，創造生動的畫面。這種技巧在旅遊寫作中很常見，能夠讓讀者身臨其境。
Imagine a city that tumbles down 45 hills toward the Pacific Ocean, where every wall is a canvas, every staircase a mosaic, and every alleyway hides a story.
使用 'Imagine a city' 開頭，然後用三個 'every...is...' 的並列結構來描繪城市的特徵。這種排比修辭法創造了強烈的節奏感和畫面感。
Imagine [a place] that [verb] [direction], where [every noun] [is/verb] [description], [every noun] [description], and [every noun] [description].
用 'Imagine' 句型描述一個你喜歡的旅遊目的地。
'Imagine a village that nestles in a green valley, where every house is covered in flowers, every street leads to a view, and every meal is a celebration.'
Active vs. Passive Voice in Descriptions ▾
主動語態和被動語態的交替使用可以豐富寫作風格。主動語態（subject + verb）更直接有力，被動語態（be + past participle）則可以強調動作的接受者。
This entire area is covered in vineyards producing famous Chilean white wines.
被動語態 'is covered in' 強調區域的狀態，而現在分詞 'producing' 則描述了葡萄園的功能。
[Subject] is covered in [noun] [verb-ing] [product].
We happily tasted some excellent Carmenere, Chardonnay, and crisp Sauvignon Blanc.
主動語態 'We happily tasted' 直接描述了他們的品酒體驗，語氣更個人化、更有參與感。
Subject + adverb + verb + [list of items].
將以下句子從被動改為主動：'The wine was tasted by us at the vineyard.'
'We tasted the wine at the vineyard.'
