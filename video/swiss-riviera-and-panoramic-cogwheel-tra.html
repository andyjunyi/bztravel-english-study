2. Montreux: The Swiss Riviera ▾
Join us today in Montreux for a lazy day on the Swiss Riviera before we catch a train up to Zermatt. Our 8-day journey crosses Switzerland by panoramic trains, stopping in breathtaking towns and alpine valleys along the way. Welcome to Montreux, where mountains meet the wild breath of Lake Geneva. Today we wander its fabled promenade — the same path that stirred artists and dreamers for generations. Here the lake acts like a vast engine of warmth: its water storing sunlight while the surrounding peaks hold back the northern cold. That fragile balance gives Montreux significantly more sunshine, and palms, magnolia, and figs thrive here at 400 m. Before us stretches Lake Geneva — 582 square kilometers of shifting light and mist, Western Europe's largest alpine lake, so large it creates its own small tides and weather. Today we explore Montreux's 7-kilometer lakefront promenade — one of Europe's most scenic, lined with historic mansions, luxury hotels, and gardens.
旅客面前的是日內瓦湖——582平方公里的光影與薄霧變幻。這是西歐最大的高山湖泊，它大到能產生自己的小幅潮汐和天氣。團隊探索了蒙特勒7公里長的湖畔步道——歐洲最美麗的步道之一，沿途是歷史悠久的豪宅、豪華酒店和面向寧靜湖面的花園。這裡的微氣候讓亞熱帶植物得以在通常無法存活的緯度茁壯成長。
3. Freddie Mercury in Montreux ▾
Freddie Mercury still stands here in bronze, fist raised towards the Alps. Montreux loved him back. Queen recorded seven albums here, and Freddie claimed this lakeside town as home — watching sunsets from his apartment above the water. What pulled Freddie here? Peace. In Montreux, he wasn't Freddie the icon — he was just another guy grabbing coffee and wandering the promenade. Locals didn't swarm him. They let him breathe. That quiet freedom meant everything. In 1991, while already fading, he recorded his last songs here. "A Winter's Tale" was his goodbye — written while staring at this same lake. He sings about the blue sky, the mountains, the stillness. He knew he wouldn't see many more winters. Yet somehow the song feels full of life.
佛萊迪·墨裘瑞的銅像仍然矗立在這裡，拳頭高舉面向阿爾卑斯山。蒙特勒也同樣愛戴他。皇后樂團在這裡錄製了七張專輯，佛萊迪將這個湖畔小鎮視為家，在水上的公寓欣賞日落。是什麼吸引了佛萊迪來到這裡？和吸引所有人的原因一樣——平靜。在蒙特勒，他不是佛萊迪巨星。他只是一個喝咖啡、在步道上漫步的普通人。當地人不會蜂擁而上。他們讓他呼吸。那份寧靜的自由彌足珍貴。
4. French Switzerland ▾
Everything is in French. The French food. We went from beer and sausages to wine and steaks. And everybody speaks French now, not German. It really feels like you're in a different country — but it's the same country. In Montreux, life gathers around the lake. The hillside city descends daily to restaurants, playgrounds, and bike paths. Some of the boats you see aren't just pleasure craft — people actually commute by boat here, crossing between Switzerland and France like others might take a bus. Our time here was short, just a stop on our rail journey, but it's unforgettable. We barely scratched the surface. Good night, everyone.
離開蒙特勒後，團隊登上前往策馬特的全景齒軌火車。火車穿過令人屏息的阿爾卑斯風景，經過葡萄園、森林和山村。齒軌技術使火車能夠應對普通火車無法處理的陡峭坡度。隨著火車攀升，景觀從地中海式的湖畔植被轉變為高山森林，最終到達構成瑞士標誌性山景的岩石和積雪山峰。
5. Zarko's Reflection on Switzerland ▾
It's beyond me why Switzerland is so prosperous. You have French people, Italians, Germans speaking different languages. And how come they don't want to be part of their respective countries? How come there are no civil wars, no civil unrest, no divisions? Everybody is living together peacefully, prosperous. Does not make sense. Everywhere around the world, even people speaking with a slight difference between two languages are fighting each other. Yet right here, there are no natural resources. Even the countryside looks landscaped. The fields, the hills look like golf courses. Everything is beautiful, clean, organized. The rest of the world can learn from Switzerland. And I never knew that a country of 8.5 million could have such a great rail system. The trains run every half hour. You can connect to pretty much any part of Switzerland — and riding on a train, looking at the roads, you don't see cars, because you don't need to drive from place to place when the trains are this efficient. Once you get to the train station, you have buses, everything is connected, everything is organized. Everything is perfect.
這段旅程是為期八天、以全景火車穿越瑞士的冒險之旅的一部分，沿途在令人屏息的小鎮和阿爾卑斯山谷停留。從棕櫚樹環繞的日內瓦湖岸到無車的策馬特街道，每一段都展現了瑞士多樣景觀的不同篇章。蒙特勒溫和近乎地中海的氣候與策馬特清新的高山空氣之間的對比，展示了瑞士在一次旅程中驚人的地理多樣性。
6. Cogwheel Train to Zermatt ▾
Leaving the Swiss Riviera behind, we roll into the Rhône Valley — Switzerland's fruit basket and wine cellar. This place gets 300 days of sun a year. Out the window: vineyards, cornfields, and small factories blur past, the rhythm of everyday Switzerland most travelers never notice. At Visp, we transferred to the Matterhorn Gotthard Bahn, starting the steep final leg towards Zermatt — a true feat of Swiss railway engineering. You can see the cog wheel in the middle of the track — it grips on that so it can pull itself up the hill. In the next hour, we rise a thousand meters through 15 tunnels and 30 bridges, defying gravity across 35 rugged kilometers — climbing straight up. As the valley narrows, steep cliffs rise on both sides, restricting sunlight so severely that some villages only get 2 hours of it in winter. We've entered Switzerland's high alpine zone, where long winters dominate the year and human life adapts carefully to extreme cold and limited daylight. Don't miss our next video. We're off to Zermatt, Switzerland's ski capital — cable cars to the top of the world, the real Matterhorn, and the Glacier Express across Switzerland to St. Moritz.

關係副詞 'where' 用來引導描述地點的形容詞子句，讓句子更優雅，避免重複使用介系詞 + which。
