7. La Paz ▾
Driving from the lake and passing through the bare highlands, we suddenly discover the chaotic, bustling edge of a truly massive city. Formed exactly like a giant bowl, it perfectly holds La Paz — the highest altitude major city in the world. The high area resting right on the rim is called El Alto and it sits over 13,600 ft. Steep and winding roads lead down to the city center where our hotel sits. La Paz features a unique vertical society — the wealthier locals live in the warmer, westernized valley bottom, while the poorer neighborhoods cling to the steep, thin air of El Alto. Since traditional subways are impossible here, they built an incredible sky subway of clean, quiet cable cars for the most spectacular daily commute on the planet.

Past Simple for Sequential Narrative ▾
使用簡單過去式來按時間順序敘述事件。這種敘事結構清楚地展示了事件的先後順序，使讀者能夠跟隨旅行者的經歷。
We just flew from Lima to Juliaca. Stepping off the plane at 12,500 ft, we immediately noticed the surprisingly thin air.
使用 'flew' 和 'noticed' 兩個簡單過去式動詞來敘述連續事件。'Stepping off' 是現在分詞短語，表示伴隨動作。
Subject + simple past + [action]. [Verb-ing] + [detail], subject + simple past + [observation].
We planned to visit, but our 1-hour flight to Juliaca took 7 hours. Thick fog forced our plane back to Lima to refuel and wait. Since we missed the islands completely, we're just having a late lunch in Puno.
混合使用過去式（planned, took, forced, missed）和現在進行式（'re having）來表達從過去到現在的時間過渡。'Since' 表示原因。
Subject + planned to [verb], but [unexpected event] happened. [Adverse condition] + forced + [result]. Since [consequence], [current situation].
用簡單過去式按時間順序描述一個旅行經歷中的三次事件。
'We arrived at the airport at 6 AM. The flight was delayed by three hours. We finally landed at noon, exhausted but excited.'
Comparative Adjectives for Contrast ▾
比較級形容詞用於對比不同的事物或狀態。影片中使用比較級來強調高海拔環境與正常環境的差異。
This rich, juicy, orange trout, surprisingly looks and tastes much more like fresh salmon.
'Much more like' 是比較級結構，將湖鱒魚的味道和口感與鮭魚進行比較。'Surprisingly' 修飾整個句子，表達意外之情。
[Subject] looks and tastes [comparative] like [something else].
用 'much more like' 比較兩個相似的事物。
'This local cheese tastes much more like the one I had in France than the processed kind from the supermarket.'
