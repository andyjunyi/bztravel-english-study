2. St. Moritz: Off-Season Exploration ▾
Welcome back to BZ Travel. We roam the world and you're part of the journey. This adventure covers Switzerland end to end — exploring St. Moritz, riding the Bernina and Gotthard trains, crossing Lake Lucerne, and concluding our travels in St. Gallen. Located high in Switzerland's Engadin Valley, St. Moritz has earned its reputation as the playground of the elite — known for world-class skiing, designer boutiques, and 300 sunny days each year. We have a full day here to explore and we're going to go up via the funicular. St. Moritz has 88 lifts covering this massive ski playground, from funiculars to high-speed chairs to button lifts for beginners. 350 km of snowpack stretching across one of the world's most famous mountain playgrounds. Right now it's the off-season — very quiet, stores are closed. But in ski season, this place is completely packed. They've had world ski championships here.
團隊在淡季——夏冬之交——造訪聖莫里茨，小鎮幾乎空無一人。大多數商店都關門了，街道很安靜。但基礎設施令人瞠目結舌：88條滑雪纜車、350公里的滑雪道，以及20分鐘內從谷底上升到2,460公尺的纜車。纜車從一條長隧道內開始，然後出現在現代化的山坡開發區和壯麗的湖景前。聖莫里茨湖在夏天變成鮮豔的藍色，冬天則完全結冰，自1907年以來舉辦賽馬活動。
3. Cable Car to Klein Matterhorn Heights ▾
The funicular connects the city's valley floor up to 2,460 m in roughly 20 minutes. It begins inside a long tunnel before emerging among new hillside developments — featuring sharp architecture and lake views. A big contrast to the region's traditional chalet style. During the winter the lifts move about 20,000 people hourly. During the off-season, it's closer to 200 per day. Outside ski season, hikers and mountain bikers take over the slopes. The current cable car system first opened in 1954 and got a serious facelift during the 1990s. Looking for a restaurant with a view? Try this — it's probably the highest place we've ever eaten. Perched on the top station, one of eastern Switzerland's highest year-round dining locations. Snowcapped peaks, a turquoise lake below, and air so clear it feels fake.
伯連納快車是一條世界聞名的全景列車，連接聖莫里茨和義大利的提拉諾。建於1908年至1910年間，沒有現代機器——只有毅力和體力——它爬升到奧斯皮齊奧伯連納的2,253公尺，是歐洲最高的鐵路交叉點之一。這列火車沒有齒輪——純靠牽引力應付7%的坡度。在高海拔地區，它穿過一片荒蕪、幾乎像月球表面的岩石區和殘雪。火車會停下來讓乘客拍照，健行步道向各個方向蜿蜒而去。
4. The Bernina Express ▾
Next up, we're hopping on the Bernina Express — a world-renowned panoramic train connecting St. Moritz to Tirano, Italy. It crosses dramatic alpine landscapes built between 1908 and 1910, no modern machines, just grit and muscle. It climbs to 2,253 m at Ospizio Bernina — one of Europe's highest crossings. No cog wheels here, just pure traction handling a 7% grade. For context, most railways panic at three percent. As the Bernina Express descends towards Italy, the scenery transitions dramatically. Stone-built villages appear, fields grow greener, and the rock strata shift to warmer reddish tones. The vegetation thickens, giving way to chestnut and deciduous trees — marking a clear ecological and cultural shift from the alpine environment of Switzerland to the Mediterranean foothills of northern Italy. Then comes the wild part: the famous Brusio spiral viaduct. A perfect stone circle that lets the Bernina Express drop altitude without going too steep — pure old-school engineering genius built long before computers. And just like that, we're in Italy. The train rolls straight through Tirano, right past a church like a streetcar. Then a two-hour wait for the Bernina Express bus. Once on board, the ride along Lake Como — tight switchbacks, mountain passes, and hairpin climbs — was wild, beautiful, and unforgettable.
接下來是哥達全景列車，穿越瑞士戲劇性的阿爾卑斯心臟地帶。旅程帶領乘客穿過91條隧道和291座橋樑，這條路線花費了超過半個世紀才開鑿出來。上阿爾卑斯山口爬升到2,033公尺，是歐洲最高的鐵路路線之一。工程設計與景觀一樣令人驚嘆。緩慢而持續的移動產生了平靜的節奏，而窗外的景色則提供了持續的視覺享受——雄偉的山脈、阿爾卑斯山谷、迷人的村莊和歷史悠久的橋樑。
5. Gotthard Panorama Express & Lake Lucerne ▾
Good morning. We are on the Gotthard Panorama Express train and we have almost floor-to-ceiling curved windows — you can see all the way up into the mountains as you go along. It's a three-hour journey from Lugano to Lucerne, then two hours on a boat crossing the lake. We're going through the Gotthard Tunnel — once the longest tunnel in the world, 15 km straight through the Alps. It took 8 years to build. Switzerland felt it couldn't connect the Mediterranean Italian-speaking side to the German-speaking side any other way. Swiss train engineering: when there's a mountain in the way, they don't go around or over it — they punch straight through. Passing the Biaschina Viaduct — unreal. The bridge clings to the cliffs for miles. It's massive, almost terrifying, and absolutely beautiful. We finished our journey on the train and now we're heading over to the boat to cross Lake Lucerne. What's really nice is they took our luggage when we checked in — we don't have to deal with it until we get on the other side of the lake. The Gotthard Panoramic Express boat is a real working boat. Locals actually use it to get between towns. Lakes of Switzerland are on another level. The mountains dive straight into the deep blue water. Meadows spill down the slopes and cows graze near storybook chalets. It looks fake — like a perfect postcard that somehow came alive.
乘船橫越盧塞恩湖，團隊從另一個角度體驗瑞士的風景。湖泊像一台巨大的溫暖引擎，儲存陽光，而周圍的山峰則阻擋了北方的寒冷。旅程在聖加侖結束，這裡有著名的修道院區和圖書館。在整個旅程中，持續的視覺刺激加上火車安靜平穩的行駛，產生了冥想般的效果。瑞士著名的全景列車確實提供了一種難忘的方式來體驗阿爾卑斯山。
6. Final Day: St. Gallen ▾
Good morning — this is our last day on our grand tour of Switzerland by train. We left Lucerne and we're heading to St. Gallen on the Voralpen-Express, 3 hours. We're going to see the longest viaduct and the highest viaduct in Switzerland, as well as the longest walking bridge. This isn't a fancy panoramic train, just a regular commuter line — no curved windows, but the same killer view. We spot the longest walkable wooden bridge, over 800 meters long, pedestrian only. Super popular with hikers, especially at sunset. We arrived at St. Gallen on a Sunday. In Switzerland on Sunday, they close everything. You're not even allowed to mow your lawn. It's a beautiful old town walking street with lovely boutique shops — and nothing is open. Unfortunately, we're here on our last day and I wanted to buy some chocolate. I'll have to buy it at the airport. The top thing to see in St. Gallen is the Abbey Library — one of the oldest libraries in the world, founded in 612. 分詞構句表達結果 — creating / producing / making + 結果 ▾
現在分詞（V-ing）在句尾可以用來表達前面整個動作所導致的結果，是英文寫作中很常見的結構。
The slow, continuous movement creates a calming rhythm, while the scenery provides constant visual interest.
→ 兩個並列的動作：創造平靜節奏 + 提供視覺趣味 → 產生的結果 = producing a meditative effect
S + V + O, creating/producing + [結果]
The train moved smoothly through the mountains, ______ (產生了放鬆的效果) on all passengers.
creating a relaxing effect
主動 vs 被動分詞 — V-ing vs p.p. 當形容詞 ▾
現在分詞（V-ing）表示主動或正在進行，過去分詞（p.p.）表示被動或已完成。需要注意分詞的正確選擇。
The surrounding homes are distinctly modern, featuring sharp architecture.
→ 'surrounding' = 正在周圍環繞的（主動） → 'newly groomed' = 剛被整理過的（被動） → 'meticulously groomed runs' = 被精心整理過的滑雪道
V-ing（主動）+ N / p.p.（被動）+ N
The ______ (被積雪覆蓋的) peaks and the ______ (冉冉升起的) sun created a beautiful scene.
snow-covered / rising
