2. Arriving in a Storm ▾
Welcome to BZ Travel. Today, we explore South America's wine capital, home of Malbec and Asado. To cross the Andes range, you can brave a risky mountain pass or face a turbulent flight. We chose the 1-hour flight from Santiago to Mendoza. We're going from the airport to our hotel and they're having a major storm here in Mendoza — with hail. We went from 36 to to 20°C. It's and it's dark out. Mendoza is a desert, one of Argentina's driest spots with only 8 inches of rain a year, so this storm is hard to believe. It feels like the whole year's worth is coming down right now. Mendoza sits at the foot of the Andes and that location makes it a global hotspot for severe hailstorms. Cold mountain air from the Andes collides with warm, humid air pushing in from the Atlantic, and the result is massive, powerful storm cells. Our guide mentioned hailstones here can reach the size of tennis balls. So locals have a simple solution: everyone keeps a heavy blanket in their car specifically to throw over it during a hailstorm.

3. Irrigation Trenches ▾
Look closely at the sidewalk edges and you'll notice something interesting. There are deep, open concrete trenches running along almost every street in the city. Most of the year, they actually carry water from the Andes snowmelt to irrigate the trees planted along every street in Mendoza. That is not a puddle, that is not flooding — that is a moving river on a city street, and those cars are just driving through it. One hour later, the street is almost dry, like it never happened.
整根肋骨仍然附著，多汁且完美火候。但最突出的是節奏和氛圍。阿根廷人不急著吃完晚餐——這是社交時間。餐廳通常要到晚上才開始有人。對我們來說，這需要一些調整——在家鄉，晚上我們已經上床睡覺了。吃完這樣的晚餐，散步正是我們需要的，而在門多薩沒有比阿里斯蒂德斯大道更適合散步的地方了。時間還算早，但大道已經活躍起來了。年輕人剛開始出門，群體聚攏，桌子慢慢被佔滿。我只能想像午夜時分這裡會是什麼樣子。我們的導遊告訴我們，那時候才是真正的高峰——餐廳、酒吧、酒館全部爆滿，而且大多數店家會營業到很晚。這條大道感覺嶄新、乾淨、燈光明亮、充滿活力。這顯然是這座城市夜晚甦醒的地方，而我們才剛剛開始。早安，門多薩。今天我們要參觀三家酒莊。沒錯，早餐品酒正式列入行程。看看街道——完全乾了。沒有水坑，沒有那場暴風雨的任何痕跡。如果我們昨天沒親眼看到，我們絕對不會相信發生過暴風雨。這是一家小型酒莊Casa Aurora，主人住在美國，是什麼金融大師之類的，但他買下這些酒莊當作興趣。他在多年廢棄之後接手了這裡。他寄了很多酒回家。真的很美。我們的第一站是一家精品酒莊——以門多薩的標準來說算是小型。這只是你在這裡能找到的一種風格。但門多薩總共有1,200家酒莊，從這些小型莊園一直到供應80個國家超市貨架的大型企業。僅這一個地區就生產了阿根廷所有葡萄酒的70%。門多薩最出名的是馬爾貝克，但他們也在這裡種植和生產許多其他品種。唯一的問題是——
4. The Argentine Asado ▾
The dining experience in Argentina is one you'll remember for a long time. Restaurants keep the kitchen completely open and the grill master will gladly show you the whole process. Long charcoal grills loaded with all kinds of meat and sausages, sizzling and filling the air with an incredible aroma. Next to the main grill, there's a dedicated charcoal oven — and it's what makes the Argentine method stand out. Most people think barbecue means lighting charcoal and cooking directly. Here they heat the coals in the oven first, then transfer them to the grill. The meat cooks over perfectly prepared heat. Dinner in Argentina is a cultural experience, not just a meal. We sat family style with our new friends. The Malbec kept flowing and the meat kept coming. I had my first Tomahawk steak here — a massive cut with the full rib bone still attached, juicy and perfectly done. But what stood out most was the pace and the atmosphere. Argentinians don't rush dinner. Restaurants here typically don't fill up until p.m. For us, that took some adjusting.
老實說，我們的身體還在要咖啡，不是葡萄酒。但我們就在這裡了。這家酒莊的主人是Peter Dartley——Pequot Capital的前副主席，這是世界上最大的對沖基金之一。他的四個葡萄園分別以他每個孫子的名字命名：Jamila、Naoki、Lauren和Owen。退休後有這樣的興趣愛好還不錯。地下室裡有一個私人酒窖——完美的溫度、完美的濕度，專為瓶陳而建。這是Peter的個人收藏，全部非賣品。他只是偶爾運一些出去用於特殊活動，或者只是為了給幾個朋友留下深刻印象。說到收藏——你收藏什麼？在下面的留言區告訴我們。我們收藏回憶。看右邊那些發酵槽——是水泥的，不是鋼的。非常大。不是大多數人想像的釀酒場景。哇，這房間真美。各位，哦，哇。太美了。準備好了嗎？好了。看來我們要品嚐三種不同的酒——一款馬爾貝克、一款調和酒、一款卡本內蘇維濃。哦，卡本內蘇維濃。現在是早上，我們實在沒什麼心情喝，但還是試試吧。好了，第一款品的是調和酒——是馬爾貝克，但來自三個不同的葡萄園。來試試吧。在這個時間品酒可不容易。但老實說，它們相當不錯。酒莊還附設零售店和餐廳。考慮到這裡曾經完全廢棄，他們所建造的一切著實令人印象深刻。門多薩有15萬公頃的葡萄園，每一座都依賴安第斯山脈的融雪水。這些水注入河流和水庫，然後通過重力供水渠道直接流入葡萄園。釀酒師只需要打開一個小閘門，水就會直接灌進來。好了，我們準備去第二家酒莊了——

Young people are just starting to come out, groups forming, tables filling up slowly. Our guide told us that's when it really peaks — restaurants, bars, and pubs completely packed, most places staying open well past midnight. This is clearly where the city comes to life at night.

6. Winery 1: Casa Aurora ▾
Good morning from Mendoza. Today we're visiting three wineries — wine tasting for breakfast is officially on the schedule. Look at the streets: completely dry. No trace of that storm. Our first stop is Casa Aurora, a boutique winery. The owner is Peter Dartley, the former vice chairman of Pequot Capital, one of the largest hedge funds in the world. His four vineyards are named Jamila, Naoki, Lauren, and Owen — after each of his grandchildren. Down in the basement is a private vault, perfect temperature and humidity, built for aging wine in the bottle. None of it is for sale. The fermentation tanks are concrete, not steel. Not what most we picture when we think winemaking. We tried three wines — a Malbec blend, and a Cabernet Sauvignon. It's a It's a Malbec, but it's a blend from three different three different vineyards. Judging wine at this hour is not easy, but honestly, they were pretty good.

7. Winery 2: Piatelli Vineyards ▾
Our second winery, Piatelli Vineyards, is a totally diffe rent feel. This winery is owned by another American, John Malinski from Minnesota. Unlike the first winery, they use a drip irrigation system here. The reservoir on the property makes everything look beautiful, and that fountain isn't just decorative — it aerates the water to keep it from going stagnant. What really stood out was the setting: beautiful landscaping, open views, birds everywhere.

8. Winery 3: Achaval Ferrer & Chamera Bistro ▾
Now we're heading to lunch at Chamera Bistro inside Achaval Ferrer, one of the most prestigious wineries in Mendoza. The owner is Yuri Shefler, a Russian-born billionaire who also owns the Stoli Group behind Stolichnaya Vodka. Our third and final winery — a four-course lunch with wine pairings for every course. Chamera Bistro serves Michelin-level presentation and taste. Honestly, one of the best lunches we've ever had. When money is no object and the passion is there, the results speak for themselves. Mendoza was incredible. But Buenos Aires is next and that city is on a completely different level. One of our favorite places in the world — see you in Buenos Aires.

Cause and Effect: 'makes it' + result ▾
使用 'makes it + noun' 結構來表達因果關係，說明某個因素導致某個結果。這種結構在說明文中特別有用。
Mendoza sits at the foot of the Andes and that location makes it a global hotspot for severe hailstorms.
'Sits at the foot of the Andes' 是原因，'makes it a global hotspot' 是結果。這種結構清晰地建立了因果關係。
[Subject] [sits/is located] [location detail] and that [characteristic] makes it [result].
Cold mountain air from the Andes collides with warm, humid air pushing in from the Atlantic and the result is massive, powerful storm cells.
先描述兩個因素（cold mountain air 和 warm humid air）的碰撞，然後用 'the result is' 引出後果。
[Factor A] collides with [Factor B] and the result is [consequence].
用 'makes it' 結構描述一個地理位置如何造成某種現象。
'The city sits in a valley surrounded by mountains, and that location makes it a hotspot for fog and mist.'

使用現在簡單式來描述一般事實、科學原理和規律性發生的事件。影片中用現在簡單式來介紹Mendoza的氣候特徵和地理現象。
