1. A City Tour With a Local Guide ▾
To truly understand the heartbeat of Lima, the travelers hired a private guide to show them the city's most famous spots. Exploring a city with a local is simply the best way to do it — that personal connection is irreplaceable. Rather than following a flag or staring out of a crowded bus window, they experienced Lima through the eyes of someone who knows the absolute ins and outs of their city. The tour began at the breathtaking Love Park, perched right on top of a cliff overlooking the Pacific Ocean.
為了真正了解利馬的脈動，旅客聘請了一位私人導遊來帶他們參觀城市最著名的景點。和當地人一起探索城市無疑是最棒的方式——那種人與人之間的連結是無可取代的。他們不是跟著旗子走，也不是擠在遊覽車上看風景，而是透過一個對城市瞭若指掌的人的眼睛來體驗利馬。旅程從令人屏息的愛情公園開始，它坐落在俯瞰太平洋的懸崖上。
2. Love Park and Miraflores ▾
Love Park, or Parque del Amor, is arguably Lima's top destination. Nestled on a cliff in the upscale Miraflores neighborhood, it offers sweeping views of the Pacific. The park features colorful undulating walls covered in beautiful mosaics, where each tile design carries a different meaning about life and romance. Lovers come here to lock padlocks on the fences and throw away the key. In the center stands a massive sculpture called El Beso — The Kiss. The nearby Miraflores district is safe, pristine, and filled with lush parks, luxury hotels, and high-end restaurants.
愛情公園可以說是利馬的頂級景點。它坐落在高檔的米拉弗洛雷斯區的懸崖上，享有太平洋的遼闊景觀。公園擁有色彩繽紛的波浪狀牆面，覆蓋著美麗的馬賽克，每個瓷磚設計都承載著關於生活和愛情的不同含義。戀人們來到這裡，在欄杆上鎖上掛鎖，然後把鑰匙丟掉。公園中央矗立著一座名為「吻」的巨大雕塑。附近的米拉弗洛雷斯區安全、純淨，充滿了綠意盎然的公園、豪華酒店和高級餐廳。
3. Barranco: The Bohemian Heart ▾
Next, they visited Barranco, the bohemian heart of Lima. This vibrant neighborhood has historically been home to the city's artists and intellectuals. The streets are packed with striking murals and cozy cafes. Every wall tells a different story. Artists paint directly on the streets to make their work completely accessible to everyone who walks by. A famous landmark here is the Puente de los Suspiros — the Bridge of Sighs. Local legend says if you can hold your breath all the way across and make a wish, that wish will come true.
接下來，他們參觀了巴蘭科——利馬的波希米亞之心。這個充滿活力的街區歷來是城市藝術家和知識分子的家園。街道上布滿了引人注目的壁畫和舒適的咖啡館。每一面牆都在訴說著不同的故事。藝術家直接在街頭作畫，讓每個路過的人都能欣賞他們的作品。這裡著名的地標是嘆息橋。當地傳說，如果你能屏住呼吸走完整座橋並許個願望，那個願望就會實現。
4. Historic Center and Grand Hotel Bolivar ▾
Continuing into the historic Lima City Center, the travelers passed the impressive Palace of Justice and the famous Grand Hotel Bolivar. This historic hotel has hosted an incredible roster of famous guests including John F. Kennedy, Richard Nixon, Ernest Hemingway, John Wayne, and Clark Gable. The lobby features intricate stonework, grand marble columns, and a spectacular stained-glass dome that filters the Peruvian sunlight beautifully. An antique reception ledger and a vintage elevator offer glimpses into a bygone era of luxury travel.
繼續深入利馬歷史中心，旅客經過了令人印象深刻的司法宮和著名的玻利瓦爾大飯店。這家歷史悠久的飯店接待過許多著名賓客，包括約翰·F·甘迺迪、理查·尼克森、厄尼斯特·海明威、約翰·韋恩和克拉克·蓋博。大廳有著精緻的石雕、宏偉的大理石柱，以及一個壯觀的彩繪玻璃穹頂，美麗地過濾了秘魯的燦爛陽光。古董登記簿和復古電梯讓人一窺那個已逝去的奢華旅行年代。
5. Pisco Tasting and Plaza de Armas ▾
No visit to Peru would be complete without trying Pisco, a distilled grape spirit. The travelers did a proper Pisco tasting, sampling different variations including the famous Pisco Sour. They then visited Plaza de Armas, the main square dating back to 1535. Surrounded by bright yellow colonial buildings with dark wooden balconies, the square is home to the Presidential Palace and Lima Cathedral, where Francisco Pizarro is buried. A massive pre-Incan adobe complex, Huaca Pucllana, stands right in the middle of the modern city — a stunning reminder of Peru's layered history.
造訪秘魯不能不品嚐皮斯可酒——一種蒸餾葡萄烈酒。旅客進行了一場正式的皮斯可品酒會，品嚐了多種不同風味，包括著名的皮斯可酸酒。接著他們參觀了武器廣場，這個主要廣場的歷史可追溯到1535年。廣場周圍是明亮的黃色殖民建築，带有深色木製陽台，廣場上有總統府和埋葬著法蘭西斯科·皮薩羅的利馬大教堂。一座巨大的前印加土坯建築群——普克拉納遺址——就矗立在現代城市中心，令人驚嘆地提醒著人們秘魯層層累積的歷史。

不定詞片語表達目的 — To truly understand..., S + V... ▾
不定詞片語放在句首可以表達目的，等於 In order to...。這是一種正式的寫作結構，用來強調行動的動機。
To truly understand the heartbeat of the culture, we hired a private guide.
→ 'To truly understand' = In order to truly understand → 句首不定詞強調目的，主句說明採取的行動
To + V..., S + V... → 「為了...，主詞做...」
______ (為了真正體驗當地生活), you should stay in a residential neighborhood.
To truly experience local life
你以為...其實不然 — You'd be forgiven for thinking that..., but... ▾
這個句型用來表達「如果某人這樣想是可以理解的」，先承認對方的合理假設，再提出反轉的事實。
You'd be forgiven for thinking it was plucked straight out of Paris.
→ 先承認建築看起來像巴黎搬來的→暗示其實是在利馬 → You'd be forgiven for = 你這樣想是可以被原諒的
You'd be forgiven for thinking + that 子句
______ (你會以為這是在歐洲) when you see the architecture.
You'd be forgiven for thinking you're in Europe
