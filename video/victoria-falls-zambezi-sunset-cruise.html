1. One of Earth's Rawest Wonders ▾
Victoria Falls is one of Earth's rawest, most violent, and most beautiful sites. The travelers stroll along the rim of the falls and later relax on a sunset cruise along the Zambezi River. Victoria Falls sits right where Zimbabwe, Zambia, and Namibia come together — nature's border and spectacular all-in-one. The city of Victoria Falls thrives under the shadow of its namesake, and this single destination pumps 15 percent into Zimbabwe's GDP.
維多利亞瀑布是地球上最原始、最猛烈、最美麗的景點之一。旅行者沿著瀑布邊緣漫步，隨後在尚比西河上享受日落遊船。維多利亞瀑布位於辛巴威、尚比亞和納米比亞的交界處——大自然與壯麗景觀的完美結合。維多利亞瀑布城在其同名瀑布的陰影下蓬勃發展，這個單一目的地為辛巴威的GDP貢獻了15%。
2. A Waterfall Unlike Any Other ▾
Victoria Falls is different from any waterfall you have ever seen. Other waterfalls tumble off cliffs, but Victoria Falls — the Zambezi River flows gently for a thousand kilometers, then drops suddenly into a crack in the Earth's crust. From a helicopter, you really see it: it does not fall over, it falls in. A chasm carved by ancient volcanoes and time, this is what sets it apart. The drop is over 100 meters straight down, and the thunder can be heard from 40 kilometers away.
維多利亞瀑布與你所見過的任何瀑布都不同。其他瀑布從懸崖邊傾瀉而下，而維多利亞瀑布——尚比西河平靜地流淌了一千公里，然後突然墜入地殼的一條裂縫中。從直升機上你才能真正看到：它不是流下來，而是流進去。一個由古代火山和時間共同雕刻而成的深淵，這就是它的與眾不同之處。落差超過100公尺，雷鳴般的轟響在40公里外都能聽到。
3. Walking Through the Rainforest of Mist ▾
On the Zimbabwean side, jungle trails lead to numbered viewpoints where you can stare directly into the wall of falling water. The mist creates a rainforest ecosystem, lush and dripping. Without a poncho, you will get completely drenched. The contrast is remarkable — everywhere else is dry and brown, but here it is a lush jungle. Standing before the falls, the narrator says, 'I've stood before many waterfalls, yet none rival the raw power of this.'
在辛巴威一側，叢林小徑通向編號的觀景點，你可以直接凝視傾瀉而下的水牆。水霧創造了一個雨林生態系統，鬱鬱蔥蔥、濕潤欲滴。沒有雨衣，你一定會全身濕透。對比非常明顯——其他地方乾燥棕黃，但這裡是茂密的叢林。站在瀑布前，敘述者說：「我去過許多瀑布，但沒有一個能與這座的原始力量相比。」
4. Sunset on the Zambezi ▾
The sunset cruise on the Zambezi Explorer is the perfect way to end the day. Glass in hand, the horizon is on fire. It is almost impossible to imagine this peaceful river soon plunging into the chaos of Victoria Falls. Wide waters stretch between Zambia and Zimbabwe, with riverfront lodges glimmering on the Zambian banks. Far ahead, the mist of Victoria Falls towers into the sky. Along the shores, hippos surface and elephants strip trees — wild Africa alive beside the calm waters.
在尚比西探險者號上的日落遊船是結束一天的完美方式。手持酒杯，地平線燃燒如火。很難想像這條平靜的河流很快就會衝入維多利亞瀑布的混亂之中。寬闊的水域在尚比亞和辛巴威之間延伸，尚比亞岸邊的河畔小屋閃爍著光芒。遠方，維多利亞瀑布的水霧聳入天際。沿岸，河馬浮出水面，大象剝食樹枝——狂野的非洲在平靜的水域旁生機勃勃。
5. The Story of Zimbabwe ▾
The video also touches on Zimbabwe's complex history. Most tourists see only two days here for the falls, but the real country is different — rich land, a broken economy, a people who have endured war and hardship. Once called Rhodesia, it was the breadbasket of Africa with productive farms. After independence in 1980, land seizures led to collapse, starvation, and hyperinflation. Zimbabwe's 100 trillion dollar note became worthless, a stark reminder of a nation's struggles beyond its natural wonders.
影片也觸及了辛巴威複雜的歷史。大多數遊客只為了瀑布在這裡待兩天，但真正的國家是不同的——土地富饒，經濟破碎，人民經歷了戰爭和苦難。曾經被稱為羅德西亞，它是非洲的糧倉，擁有富饒的農場。1980年獨立後，土地掠奪導致了崩潰、飢荒和惡性通貨膨脹。辛巴威的100兆元紙幣變得一文不值，這是一個國家在其自然奇觀之外掙扎的嚴酷提醒。

比較否定結構 — No/none/nothing + 比較級 ▾
使用否定詞搭配比較級可以表達最高級的強調效果。'None rival the raw power' 比 'It is the most powerful' 更有修辭力量。
I've stood before many waterfalls, yet none rival the raw power of this.
→ 'none rival' = 沒有一個能比得上 → 'yet' 連接前後對比，加強語氣
None rival + 名詞 → "沒有任何事物能與...相比"
It doesn't fall over, it falls in.
→ 'doesn't fall over, it falls in' = 不是流下來，而是流進去 → 否定—肯定對比結構，強調獨特性
It doesn't [A], it [B] → "不是A，而是B"
I have traveled to many countries, yet ______ (沒有能與日本之美相比).
none rival the beauty of Japan
such...that / so...that 因果強調句 ▾
使用 'so + 形容詞 + that' 或 'such + 名詞 + that' 可以表達某種程度導致的結果。
The thunder that follows is so immense it can be heard from 40 kilometers away.
→ 'so immense...it can be heard' = 如此巨大以至於能聽到 → that 被省略了，口語中常見
S + be + so + adj + (that) + 結果子句 → "如此...以至於..."
The mist from the falls was ______ (如此濃密以至於我們看不到彼此).
so thick that we couldn't see each other
