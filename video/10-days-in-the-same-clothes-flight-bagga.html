2. Introduction: Leaving Bulgaria ▾
Welcome back to BZ Travel. It's 2026 and we're escaping the freezing Bulgarian winter for an epic South American adventure. But getting out of snowy Sofia turned into quite the frozen challenge. Let the journey begin. We traded snow for sunshine, ancient ruins, and vibrant cultures. Over the next few weeks, we're taking you along to explore the breathtaking heights of Machu Picchu, the colorful streets of Chile, Tango and Buenos Aires, and the thundering waters of Iguazú Falls. But before we could enjoy these incredible sites, we had to survive delayed connecting flights, endless layovers, and the ultimate worldwide travel nightmare — losing our luggage.
在結冰的停機坪上困了幾個小時後，飛機終於起飛前往阿姆斯特丹。抵達史基浦機場時，殘酷的現實迎面襲來——他們完全錯過了前往利馬的轉機。在服務櫃檯，一位地勤人員花了一個小時尋找替代方案。選擇有限：他們可以在貴賓室等七個小時，然後搭乘荷航到馬德里轉歐羅巴航空，再飛往利馬——為了抵達南美洲，他們得先繞回歐洲。學到一課：永遠要在旅行團出發前一天抵達，特別是在冬天。
3. Stuck on the Tarmac in Sofia ▾
Our flight was supposed to leave an hour and a half ago from Sofia, but it's snowing. But that's not the main problem. The main problem is the conveyor belt that hooks to the plane is stuck and they can't get it out. So we have to wait until they can fix that. Then after that, we have to get in the queue for de-icing. Then we take off. We've already missed our connecting flight at this point to Lima from Amsterdam. There's a possibility we could miss our National Geographic tour from Lima to Cusco. After hours stuck on the icy tarmac dealing with the broken luggage conveyor belt and endless winter de-icing, our plane had finally lifted off. Next stop: Amsterdam.
經過25小時的旅行和多次轉換航空公司後，他們終於降落在利馬——筋疲力竭但仍滿懷希望。但站在空蕩蕩的行李轉盤前，令人心碎。他們的行李沒有抵達。儘管在服務櫃檯花了兩個小時，地勤人員因為多次轉機而無法追蹤到行李。崔莎崩潰了——她無法想像沒有行李要怎麼走完秘魯、玻利維亞、智利、阿根廷和巴西的多國行程。他們兩手空空地離開了機場。
4. Amsterdam: Missed Connection ▾
Well, we made it to Amsterdam and unfortunately we did miss our connecting flight to Lima. Reality hit hard. We had to change our flight because we missed our connecting flight. So we're going to be arriving on a different airline and time into Lima. We need to let our transfer know and also our hotel. After about an hour working with a guy at the service desk, we had a couple of choices. The first option was to go the following day on the same flight and be put up in a hotel — but there weren't any business class seats available. So he spent an hour finding a flight that would work for us. He gave us the option of waiting here for 7 hours in the business lounge, then switching from KLM to Air Europa for a 2.5-hour flight to Madrid. Then a 2.5-hour layover in Madrid, followed by a direct 12.5-hour flight to Lima. It's a long journey, but we have business class so we can lay down and sleep. Glass of wine and pass out.
回到米拉弗洛雷斯的飯店，他們醒來時希望行李已經在夜間送達。但並沒有。航空公司只接受WhatsApp訊息而不接聽語音電話，讓溝通變得異常緩慢。日復一日，行李始終沒有送達。連續十天，他們穿著同樣的衣服。影片記錄了挫折感、無盡的等待，以及當一切出錯時旅行者不得不採取的創意解決方案。這是一個對長途旅行挑戰的真實寫照。
5. Travel Lesson Learned ▾
This is a lesson learned. We generally do travel a day before a tour, but you never know what could happen — especially in winter. We have friends who actually missed their cruise because they traveled the same day. Always go a day before. Don't count on specific times when traveling in winter in Europe.
儘管開局如同噩夢，團隊仍然堅持前進。這部影片是給每位旅行者的警示：永遠在行程前一天出發、絕對不要把貴重物品全部託運、隨身行李要帶一套換洗衣物，並為突發狀況做好準備。歐洲冬季旅行難以預測——行程隨時可能被打亂。但冒險仍在繼續，因為對這些旅行者來說，即使行李遺失也無法阻止他們體驗南美洲的奇妙。

After all those different airline changes, we're not sure our luggage is going to make it. Staring at an empty carousel is heartbreaking. After 36 hours of travel, we left the airport empty-handed. I told Zarko I can't do a tour of Peru, Bolivia, Chile, Argentina, and Brazil without luggage. I just can't. And tomorrow morning we're departing for Cusco.

被動語態被省略的用法 — S + be + supposed to + V ▾
Be supposed to 表示「應該、按理說要」，用來描述根據計劃或期望應該發生但可能沒有發生的事情。否定式 be not supposed to 表示「不應該」。
Our flight was supposed to leave an hour and a half ago.
→ 'was supposed to leave' = 按理說應該在一個半小時前起飛 → 暗示實際上還沒有起飛
S + be (not) supposed to + V → 「(不)應該/按理說要...」
The train ______ (應該在十分鐘前到達), but it's still not here.
was supposed to arrive ten minutes ago
讓步與對比 — It is what it is ▾
'It is what it is' 是一個常見的英文慣用語，用來接受無法改變的現實情況，帶有「既然無法改變，就接受吧」的無奈感。
Not every time goes perfect, but it is what it is. Eventually we'll get our luggage.
→ 'It is what it is' 表達接受現狀 → 雖然無奈但保持樂觀
It is what it is. → 「事情就是這樣。算了。」
The flight got canceled, but ______ (算了，接受吧). We'll try again tomorrow.
it is what it is
