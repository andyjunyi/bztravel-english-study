秘魯的聖谷曾是印加世界的心臟。這片肥沃的山谷位於庫斯科和馬丘比丘之間，是印加帝國的農業、精神和政治中心。旅客從利馬飛到庫斯科，再搭巴士直達山谷。由於山谷海拔比庫斯科低，這裡是適應高海拔環境的絕佳地點。旅程從探索古老的梯田農場和小徑開始，這些曾經養活了一個帝國。
2. Introduction: Cusco to the Sacred Valley ▾
We spend today in Peru's Sacred Valley, once the heart of the Inca world. We sip chicha made from corn and face their tradition of roasted guinea pig. Then we continue to Cusco, the former capital reshaped by Spanish rule. We flew from Lima to Cusco and then took a bus straight to the Sacred Valley. Because the valley is lower in elevation than Cusco, it's a great spot to acclimatize. Driving past Andean farmlands reveals Quechua communities — locals wearing bright handwoven alpaca garments and traditional bowler hats, preserving customs entirely distinct from ours. We started at Moray to see these huge sunken terraces. The Incas engineered this spot for farming experiments — temperatures change drastically across the different stone levels, helping specific plants grow. Heading toward the historic site of Pisac, you quickly realize why corn was vital to the Incas. The valley floor is blanketed in cornfields, and ancient stone terraces line the mountainsides. Amazingly, locals still use them.
在莫雷，團隊發現了巨大的下沉式梯田，這是印加人為農業實驗而設計的。不同石層的溫度差異極大，使特定作物能在微氣候中茁壯成長。前往皮薩克歷史遺址的路上，谷地鋪滿了玉米田，古老石梯田隨處可見，沿著山坡排列。令人驚嘆的是，當地農民至今仍在使用這些梯田，就像他們的祖先數百年前所做的那樣。
3. Hiking Pisac ▾
The hike up to the very top is a bit tough and leaves you breathless, but just look at these amazing views. At the top, we found the llamas resting, and the little baby is just adorable. The terraces sweep beautifully along the mountain's natural contours. It's one of the best-preserved spots in the Andes Mountains. Halfway up, there was a covered area where there's all these llamas. The baby was two months old. When I put my hand on the llama, I was shocked — my hand sank so far down. There's a lot of thick wool on those alpacas.
往山谷下行，下一站是馬拉斯鹽田——一個擁有數千個梯田鹽池的令人難以置信的遺址。當地人仍然使用早在印加帝國之前就建立的完全相同的方法來採收這種珍貴的粉紅鹽。他們將天然含鹽的地下泉水引入淺盆中。在旱季，強烈的安第斯陽光蒸發水分，留下結晶鹽以供採集。這種富含礦物質的鹽以其獨特風味聞名全球。
4. The Potato Farm & Wool Weaving ▾
We are climbing to 14,000 ft now, heading toward a high-altitude potato farm. Peru is actually the birthplace of this global crop. At this farm, we meet an indigenous community that's been cultivating these crops for millennia. We were shocked to discover Peru actually has over 4,000 registered potato varieties. To protect this incredible biodiversity, this community even sends their unique seeds all the way to the famous global vault in Norway for preservation. It was fascinating to see how they wash, dye, and weave wool using methods passed down for centuries. The result is pure art, totally different from the mass-produced synthetic fabrics that we wear every day. Alpaca fiber feels incredibly soft — especially the first shearing, known as baby alpaca. Because llama wool is much coarser and more durable, locals typically use it for weaving sturdy rugs. To help preserve their culture, we bought some incredible pieces. Zarko found a scarf and I got a beautiful table runner.
開車經過秘魯鄉村社區時，旅客看到木桿上掛著紅色塑膠袋——這是當地酒館的傳統標誌。這些質樸的小酒館供應奇恰酒，一種用發酵玉米釀造的古老原住民啤酒。在一間極具特色的酒館裡，天竺鼠在泥土地上到處奔跑，當地人將牠們整隻烤熟，視為珍饈。旅客也參觀了庫斯科熙攘的市場，各式各樣的馬鈴薯堆積如山——提醒著人們祕魯是這種全球作物的發源地。
5. Maras Salt Pools ▾
Descending toward the valley, our next stop was the Salinas de Maras — an incredible site featuring thousands of terrace salt pools. Locals still use the exact same methods established long before the Inca Empire to harvest this highly prized pink salt. They channel a naturally salty subterranean spring into these shallow basins. During the dry season, the intense sun evaporates the water, leaving behind crystallized salt ready for collection. This mineral-rich Maras salt is famous globally for its unique flavor and health benefits.
回到庫斯科——秘魯的古都，團隊探索了殖民時期建築、購買紀念品，並像當地人一樣搭乘公共交通。在庫斯科以北，他們參觀了一家使用當地有機食材的巧克力工廠。最精彩的是造訪一個海拔12,000英尺的羊駝和美洲駝農場。能夠近距離走路、餵食和撫摸這些可愛的動物，是一次難以置信的體驗。一隻剛出生兩小時的小美洲駝雖然腳步還有些搖晃，但已經本能地跟隨著母親——這是穿越聖谷這段難忘旅程的完美句點。
6. Chicha Tavern & Guinea Pig ▾
Driving through rural Peruvian communities, you might spot a red plastic bag hanging on a wooden pole — the traditional sign advertising the local chicha tavern inside. These rustic taverns serve chicha de jora, an ancient indigenous beer brewed from fermented corn. Inside this incredibly unique tavern, guinea pigs run everywhere across the dirt floor. They quickly drink the chicha we ceremonially pour on the ground. Locals actually roast these little animals whole — they consider them a massive delicacy here in Peru. Honestly, I'm not quite ready to try eating them.

7. Cusco Market & Chocolate Museum ▾
We're back in Cusco, the ancient capital of Peru. After checking into our hotel, we headed straight down to the bustling local market — absolutely packed with massive piles of tropical fruits and vegetables. This huge market is neatly divided into different sections. The potato stalls are definitely the most impressive thing to see. Vendors peel potatoes while waiting for customers — some people prefer buying them already peeled and ready to cook. To experience authentic everyday life in Cusco, skip the expensive taxis and take public transport instead. Just north of Cusco, the high-altitude forest produces exceptionally fine aromatic cacao beans. We visited a small-scale factory and chocolate museum and observed their entire crafting process. This boutique operation relies entirely on locally sourced organic ingredients, including that famous pink Maras salt. We first came here on our world cruise and saw the museum, the cathedral, and other spots. But today, we're just taking things easy — enjoying a rainy stroll through the historical center, admiring the amazing colonial architecture, and doing a little souvenir shopping.

分詞片語作原因/時間副詞 (V-ing / Having + p.p.) ▾
分詞片語可以簡化副詞子句。當主句與從句主詞相同時，可以將從句的動詞改為 V-ing 形式。如果動作發生在主句之前，則用 Having + p.p.。
Because the valley is lower in elevation than Cusco, it's a great spot to acclimatize.
→ Being lower in elevation than Cusco, it's a great spot to acclimatize. → 'Being' 簡化了 'Because it is' 所表達的原因關係
Being + Adj..., S + V → 「因為...，所以...」
Descending toward the valley, our next stop was Salinas de Maras.
→ 'Descending' = As we descended... → 分詞片語表示時間上的伴隨動作
V-ing + ..., S + V → 「在...的過程中，...」
______ (因為比庫斯科低), the Sacred Valley is ideal for acclimatization.
Being lower in elevation than Cusco
讓步與對比 — ...while... / while others... ▾
While 可以用來表達對比，意思是「而、然而」，連接兩個形成對比的子句。
Some look perfectly braided while others are short and curly.
→ 'while' 在此表示對比（而），不是時間上的「當...時」 → 用 two-hour-old 和 instinctively follows 形成對比
Some + V..., while others + V... → 「有些...，而其他...」
Some tourists take taxis, ______ (而其他人則搭公車) like locals.
while others take the bus
S + V + Adj + enough to + V (足夠...去做...) ▾
用 'enough to' 結構表達達到某種程度以完成某動作。形容詞放在 enough 前面，後面接不定詞。
The little llama is struggling slightly, but amazingly can stand, walk, and instinctively follows its mother just so soon after birth.
→ 雖然句中沒有直接使用 'enough'，但概念是 'old enough to stand and walk' → 'instinctively follows' 表示與生俱來的能力
S + be + Adj + enough + to V → 「...足夠...以至於能...」
The valley is low enough ______ (讓旅客適應高山環境).
for travelers to acclimatize
